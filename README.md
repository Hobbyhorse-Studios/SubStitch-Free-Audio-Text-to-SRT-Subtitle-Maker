SubStitch

Free Audio & Text to SRT Subtitle Maker

You've got an audio file. You've got the script someone read from. You just want accurate subtitles without paying $100 for a bloated studio suite that does a hundred things you'll never touch.

That's the whole reason SubStitch exists.

Drop in your audio, paste (or upload) the transcript, and SubStitch quickly times them together and hands you a ready-to-use .srt file. No spying. No accounts. No subscriptions. No uploads — everything runs right in your browser, so your audio never leaves your machine. Free, and meant to stay that way.

How it works

Drop in an MP3 (or WAV, M4A, OGG).
Paste or load the transcript — the words actually spoken in the audio.
Set how many characters you want per caption (it never splits a word).
Hit Generate, then download your .srt.
That's it. Takes about thirty seconds.

What it's good at SubStitch estimates timing across the length of your audio, weighted by word length and natural pauses. For steady narration — voiceovers, podcasts, explainers, audiobooks — it lands tight and clean. It's built for the people who just need working subtitles, not a film-studio control panel.

A couple of honest notes The timing is smart estimation, not frame-perfect AI alignment, so it shines on continuous speech and drifts a little across long silences or music gaps. Glance at your first and last captions and you're set. And the cleaner your transcript (no stage directions, no "[music]"), the tighter the result.

Free, and yours to tinker with SubStitch is free under the MIT license. The entire thing is a single HTML file — open it in any text editor and the whole works is right there, comments and all. Want to fork it, improve it, build something better on top of it? Please do. That's the point.
